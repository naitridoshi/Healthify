Prevention is better than cure.

Doctors to consult:
Pulmonologist


Diet recommended:

  • Foods rich in vitamin C- Vitamin C, a great antioxidant for the body, is abundant in oranges. Additionally, vitamin C strengthens the immune system.
  • Cruciferous vegetables- Cruciferous vegetables more popularly known as green leafy vegetables are incredibly nutrient-dense and aid in the recovery of this respiratory infection.
  • Whole grains- Be sure to eat a lot of carbohydrates. Foods like quinoa, brown rice, and oats are a great addition to the diet.
  • Protein-rich foods- Nuts, peanuts, beans, white meat, and cold water fish are examples of foods high in protein
  • Yogurt- It is well known that yogurt contains healthy bacteria that are advantageous to the body.


Food to avoid:

  • Processed Meats -- According to studies, the nitrites used in the production and preservation of processed meats may cause inflammation and stress in the lungs.
  • Alcohol- Alcohol contains sulphites, which can exacerbate the symptoms of pneumonia. Also, ethanol commonly found in beer, wine, and liquor affects lung cells.
  • Sugary Drinks- According to a study, adults who consume sweetened soft drinks more than 5 times a week are twice likely to have chronic bronchitis. While children are more likely to have asthma. Both chronic bronchitis and asthma can worsen your pneumonia symptoms.
  • Foods High in Sodium- Consuming too much table salt or salty packaged foods can aggravate the symptoms of pneumonia like shortness of breath.

Lifestyle to maintain:

  1. Practice good hygiene: Wash your hands regularly before and after having meals and protect yourself against respiratory infections.
  2. Quit smoking and adopt healthy habits: Smoking can damage your lungs natural defences against respiratory infections.
  3. Exercise regularly: to keep your immune system strong. Practising yoga and deep breathing exercises to keep your lungs healthy.
  4. Get 7-8 hours of sleep: regularly and adequate rest.
  5. Consume a balanced diet: rich in fruits and vegetables, whole grains, proteins, and nutrients, and drink lots of fluid.
