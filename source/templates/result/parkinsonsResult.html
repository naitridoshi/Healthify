Prevention is better than cure.

Doctors to consult:
Neurologist


Diet recommended:

  • Antioxidants
  • Nuts, such as walnuts, Brazil nuts, pecans, and pistachios
  • Berries, such as blueberries, blackberries, goji berries, cranberries, and elderberries
  • Nightshade vegetables, such as tomatoes, peppers, and eggplant
  • Leafy green vegetables, such as spinach and kale


Food to avoid:

  • food high in saturated fats, such as beef lard,butter,cheese,palm oil
  • Processed food, such as such as canned foods,fried foods, and regular and diet sodas
  • Foods that are hard to chew, such as white rice, tortillas, breads and pasta - especially those made with white flour
  • Starchy vegetables, Choosing foods that are easy to chew and swallow may be important, as may working with a speech language therapist.

Lifestyle to maintain:

  1. Drink plenty of water.:Staying hydrated is especially important for people with Parkinsons, who often dont experience typical thirst sensations. Aim to drink 6-8 full glasses (1.2-1.6 liters) of water each day to feel your best .
  2. Spend time outside.:Vitamin D has been demonstrated to protect against Parkinsons, so getting fresh air and sunshine may ease your symptoms
  3. Get moving.:Various kinds of exercise and physical therapy may improve your abilities and slow the progression of Parkinsons
  4. Stay mentally active: Engaging in mentally stimulating activities, such as reading,playing games, and learning new skills, has been shown to help maintain cognitive function and reduce the risk of Parkinsons disease.
  5. Stop smoking:Diabetes makes you more likely to have health problems like heart disease, eye disease, stroke, kidney disease, blood vessel disease, nerve damage, and foot problems.
  6. Maintain social connections: Maintaining strong social connections can help reduce the risk of Parkinsons disease. Social isolation has been linked to an increased risk of cognitive decline and other health problems

!! Keep in mind that certain foods and supplements may interfere with prescription drugs for this disease, so make sure you consult your doctor before changing your treatment routine. !!
